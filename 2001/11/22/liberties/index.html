Liberties?

— 22nd November 2001

I was just reading this story and thinking. Governments are doing all these things at the moment for the sake of protecting us and our way of life. “The price of freedom is eternal vigilance” they say, but what if their vigilance is restricting the freedoms of the people they’re trying to protect? Does that mean that the price of freedom is a lack of freedom?
